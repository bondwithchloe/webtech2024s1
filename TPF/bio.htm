2003

I was born in Paris, France, on July 17, 2003. I don’t have memories from that time, but I’ve heard I was a very chubby baby, nicknamed "the buddha baby" by our neighbors. My mom, an incredible woman, ran a kindergarten in our basement, where my sister Tia, our neighbors, and I learned to laugh, cry, walk and talk together.

2008

In 2008, I moved from Paris to Beirut, Lebanon. It was a big change. I was the new girl with a French accent, and my cousin was my only friend. That year, I started playing guitar, improved my piano skills, began skiing, and took up tennis. It was a year of self-discovery.

2010

By the time I was 7 I was fluent in three languages, three instruments: piano, guitar, drums, and three sports: tennis, skiing, Taekwon-do

2013

At 10, I joined the Girl Scouts, not realizing how much it would influence who I would become. I was a Girl Scout for eight years, learning leadership, teamwork, resilience, wilderness survival, and problem-solving under pressure. And a great bonus: I learned how to build cool structures like this one.

2018

At 14, after 6 weeks at Stanford I published a paper about utilizing nanobiomaterials found in butterfly wings to solve glaucoma

2019

At 15, inspired by my dad's complaints about the inefficiency of solar panels, I founded the Beirut Biomimics at my high school to improve solar technology using biomimicry. By 16, I was the youngest presenter at the World Biodiversity Forum in Davos.

2020

At 16, I launched the Impossible Lebanon Challenge, prompting high school students nationwide to address Lebanon's most pressing challenges. This led to the creation of a waste-to-energy biogarden, a mental health non-profit, and an innovative composting process inspired by ants.

2021

17, bittersweet. It was time to say goodbye to everything and everyone I knew. But, it was the start of an incredible journey at...Babson College

2022

By 18, I secured my dream internship with a women-led impact investing firm, setting the stage for my own venture.

2023

At 19, I founded my company, led an all-female team, won three pitch competitions, and gained 900 sign-ups for our MVP within two weeks. I also earned my skydiving license.

2024

At 20, I raised $85,000, won multiple pitch contests, was featured in Babson's magazine, and built a profitable startup with a thriving community of over 50 female founders.

2028

By 25, I aim to make BOND the leading leadership training tool globally, start a firm investing in female-led ventures, and skydive in over 50 different drop zones.
